THE KEY TRIANGLE

TRIANGULAR HIGHLIGHT IS THE BASIS OF GOOD PORTRAIT LIGHTING

USING THE KEY TRIANGLE

All we have to do is move the light around until we see a triangular-shaped highlight on our subject’s face. The base of the Key triangle should run through the eye, and its point should extend down the cheek approximately to the lip line.

BROAD LIGHTING / SHORT LIGHTING

A main light on the same side as the visible ear is called broad lighting. Positioning the main light on the side opposite your subject’s visible ear produces short lighting. (Whether the hair covers the “visible” ear has nothing to do with which side of the face we are talking about.)
